Vehicle Miles Traveled (VMT) by Vehicle Type – United States

Vehicle Miles Traveled (VMT) measures the total miles driven by all vehicles on U.S. roads. The Federal Highway Administration (FHWA) publishes annual VMT by vehicle class in Table VM‑1. The latest full-year dataset (2024) shows the following breakdown:

1. Light‑Duty Vehicles (Short Wheelbase)

Includes passenger cars and small SUVs.
2024 VMT: 2,222,415 million miles
Share of Total: 67.5%

2. Light‑Duty Vehicles (Long Wheelbase)

Includes pickups, large SUVs, and vans.
2024 VMT: 701,693 million miles
Share of Total: 21.3%

3. Motorcycles

Highly seasonal and a small share of total travel.
2024 VMT: 22,241 million miles
Share of Total: 0.7%

4. Buses

Includes transit, school buses, and intercity buses.
2024 VMT: 18,083 million miles
Share of Total: 0.5%

5. Single‑Unit Trucks

Local freight, construction, utilities, and service fleets.
2024 VMT: 137,080 million miles
Share of Total: 4.2%

6. Combination Trucks (Tractor‑Trailers)

Long‑haul freight and supply‑chain transport.
2024 VMT: 192,520 million miles
Share of Total: 5.8%

Total U.S. VMT (All Vehicle Types)

2024 Total: 3,294,031 million miles (≈ 3.29 trillion miles)

Interpretation

  • Light‑duty vehicles dominate U.S. travel and fuel demand.
  • Combination trucks represent only ~6% of VMT but consume a large share of diesel fuel.
  • Single‑unit trucks correlate strongly with local economic activity.
  • Buses and motorcycles contribute minimally to total VMT.
