Marine Fuel (IMO 2020)

Marine fuels must comply with IMO 2020 sulfur limits.

Fuel TypeSulfur Limit
VLSFO0.50% max
ULSFO0.10% max (ECA zones)
HSFO3.50% max (allowed only with scrubbers)

ASTM & ISO Fuel Standards

Key international and U.S. standards governing gasoline, diesel, and marine fuels.

StandardApplies ToDescription
ASTM D4814 Gasoline Defines volatility, distillation, RVP, oxygenates, and seasonal gasoline classes.
ASTM D975 Diesel (ULSD) Specifies sulfur, cetane, lubricity, cloud point, and distillation for diesel fuels.
ASTM D6751 Biodiesel (B100) Defines purity, glycerin, sulfur, and stability requirements for biodiesel blends.
ISO 8217 Marine Fuels Global marine fuel standard covering VLSFO, ULSFO, HSFO, viscosity, sulfur, and contaminants.
ISO 22241 DEF / AdBlue Specifies urea concentration, purity, and handling requirements for diesel exhaust fluid.

EPA & IMO Regulatory Limits

Key environmental and emissions regulations affecting transportation fuels.

RegulationApplies ToLimit / Requirement
EPA Tier 3 Gasoline Sulfur Gasoline 10 ppm sulfur average nationwide.
EPA ULSD Rule Diesel 15 ppm sulfur max for all on-road diesel.
EPA RFS (Renewable Fuel Standard) Gasoline & Diesel Annual renewable volume obligations (ethanol, biodiesel, advanced biofuels).
IMO 2020 Global Sulfur Cap Marine Fuels 0.50% sulfur max globally.
IMO ECA Sulfur Limit Marine Fuels 0.10% sulfur max in Emission Control Areas.
MARPOL Annex VI NOx Tier III Marine Engines 80% NOx reduction for new ships in ECAs.
