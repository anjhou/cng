  • ASTM standards are widely used to define quality, testing, and handling requirements for industrial chemicals.
  • Acid chemical standards include ASTM E223 for sulfuric acid, ASTM E1584 for nitric acid, and ASTM D515 for phosphoric acid.
  • Petrochemical feedstock standards include ASTM D2359 for benzene and ASTM D841 for toluene.
  • ASTM D843 for xylene and ASTM D2827 for styrene are commonly referenced in petrochemical manufacturing and trading.
  • Polymer and plastic material specifications commonly use ASTM D4976 for polyethylene and ASTM D4101 for polypropylene.
  • Industrial solvent and glycol standards include ASTM D1152 for methanol and ASTM D2693 for ethylene glycol.
  • Chlor-alkali chemical industries commonly reference ASTM E291 for caustic soda and ASTM E412 for chlorine.
  • ASTM specifications typically include product specifications, purity limits, and analytical testing procedures.
  • Many ASTM standards also define sampling methods, material testing requirements, and safety handling guidelines.
  • Industrial chemicals are often governed by multiple standards organizations including ASTM, ISO, API, GPA, ACS, and USP/FCC pharmaceutical specifications.

Production Pathway

Production Pathway.

Chemical Production Pathway
Sulfuric Acid Elemental sulfur → Sulfur burning → SO₂ → Catalytic oxidation (V₂O₅) → SO₃ → Absorption in H₂SO₄
Ethylene Steam cracking of ethane/propane/naphtha → Quench → Compression → Cryogenic separation
Ammonia Natural gas reforming → Syngas (H₂ + N₂) → Haber-Bosch synthesis
Urea Ammonia + CO₂ → Ammonium carbamate → Dehydration → Urea
Propylene Steam cracking (co-product) OR FCC propylene recovery OR Propane dehydrogenation (PDH)
Polyethylene Ethylene polymerization (Ziegler–Natta, Chromium, or Metallocene catalysts)
Methanol Syngas (CO + H₂) from natural gas reforming → Catalytic synthesis (Cu/ZnO/Al₂O₃)
Polypropylene Propylene polymerization (Ziegler–Natta or Metallocene catalysts)
Chlorine Chlor-alkali electrolysis of brine → Cl₂ + NaOH + H₂
Caustic Soda (NaOH) Chlor-alkali electrolysis of brine → NaOH + Cl₂ + H₂
Nitric Acid Ammonia oxidation (Ostwald process) → NO → NO₂ → Absorption in water → HNO₃
Xylene (Mixed) Catalytic reforming of naphtha → BTX extraction → Fractionation
Phosphoric Acid Wet process: Phosphate rock + H₂SO₄ → H₃PO₄ + gypsum OR Thermal oxidation of elemental phosphorus
Benzene Catalytic reforming, steam cracking pyrolysis gasoline extraction, or toluene hydrodealkylation
Toluene Catalytic reforming of naphtha → BTX extraction
Ethylene Glycol (MEG) Ethylene → Ethylene oxide → Hydration → MEG
Styrene Ethylbenzene synthesis (benzene + ethylene) → Dehydrogenation → Styrene
Formaldehyde Methanol oxidation (silver catalyst or metal oxide process) → Formaldehyde
  • Most bulk petrochemicals such as ethylene, propylene, benzene, toluene, and xylene are produced from crude oil refining and natural gas processing.
  • Steam cracking of ethane, propane, and naphtha is the primary production pathway for ethylene and propylene.
  • Methanol is mainly produced through catalytic synthesis of syngas generated from natural gas, coal, or biomass feedstocks.
  • Ammonia production primarily uses the Haber-Bosch process with hydrogen derived from steam methane reforming of natural gas.
  • Urea is manufactured by reacting ammonia with carbon dioxide under high pressure and temperature conditions.
  • Sulfuric acid is produced mainly through the Contact Process by oxidizing sulfur dioxide into sulfur trioxide followed by hydration.
  • Chlorine and caustic soda are co-produced through the chlor-alkali electrolysis process using brine as the feedstock.
  • Polyethylene and polypropylene are produced through polymerization of ethylene and propylene monomers using catalytic reactors.
  • Styrene is commonly manufactured by dehydrogenation of ethylbenzene, while ethylene glycol is produced from ethylene oxide hydration.
  • Green and low-carbon production pathways are expanding through renewable hydrogen, biomass feedstocks, carbon capture, and electrification technologies.
