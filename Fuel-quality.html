Fuel Quality Specifications

This page summarizes fuel quality requirements for refined transportation fuels: gasoline, ultra-low sulfur diesel (ULSD), and marine bunker fuel oils. Values are aligned with commonly applied standards and regulatory frameworks and are intended as engineering-reference ranges.

Gasoline (ASTM D4814 – U.S. Market)

Gasoline quality in the U.S. is governed primarily by ASTM D4814 and EPA Tier 3 regulations. The standard defines volatility classes, allowable oxygenates, and limits on contaminants to ensure drivability, emissions control, and engine protection.

  • Octane (AKI): Regular 87, Mid‑grade 89–90, Premium 91–94.
  • Sulfur: Tier 3 gasoline sulfur cap of approximately 10 ppm average.
  • Reid Vapor Pressure (RVP): Seasonal and regional limits.
  • Distillation curve: ASTM D86 limits for drivability.
  • Oxygenates: Ethanol up to 10% (E10) or 15% (E15).
  • Contaminants: Limits on gum, sulfur, and metals.

Ultra-Low Sulfur Diesel (ULSD – ASTM D975)

ULSD quality is defined by ASTM D975 and U.S. diesel regulations. The specification focuses on sulfur reduction, ignition quality, lubricity, and cold‑flow behavior.

  • Sulfur: ≤ 15 ppm.
  • Cetane number: ≥ 40.
  • Lubricity: HFRR wear scar ≤ 520 µm.
  • Water & sediment: Tight limits.
  • Cold flow properties: Seasonal cloud point & CFPP.
  • Density & viscosity: Controlled ranges.

Marine Bunker Fuel Oil (ISO 8217)

Marine fuels are specified by ISO 8217, which defines quality requirements for distillate and residual grades used in marine engines and boilers.

Key Marine Fuel Categories

  • MGO / MDO: Distillate fuels.
  • IFO 180/380/500: Residual blends.
  • VLSFO 0.5%: IMO 2020 compliant.
  • HSFO 3.5%: For scrubber‑equipped vessels.

Typical ISO 8217 Quality Parameters

  • Sulfur: 0.5% global cap; 0.1% in ECAs; up to 3.5% with scrubbers.
  • Viscosity: Grade‑dependent; RMG 380 ≈ 380 cSt at 50°C.
  • Stability & compatibility: Total sediment limits.
  • Contaminants: Water, ash, catalytic fines (Al+Si).
  • Acidity: Must be free of inorganic acids.
  • Bio‑components: FAME reporting in ISO 8217:2024.
  • Net heat of combustion: Minimum energy content.
